Overview

Purpose

Have you ever just wanted to go out into nature? Get away from civilization and just climb a mountain? Well, you’re not alone. All throughout Idaho there are hiking trails meant for people like you. But how do you find them? There are so many! Well, my website is intended to help solve this problem. With a simplistic design, this website will display several options of good hikes near you, so that you can make the right choice for your Saturday morning adventure!

Audience

this website is intended for an audience of people who enjoy to hike but aren't sure where they should got to hike in Idaho. Likely this is because they are new to the area, are students, or have just picked up the hobby

Content

Home page

Here on “Hike Idaho” we recognize that hiking is a healthy, fun, and empowering activity. While the biggest hikes might not be the best for the beginner, there is a hike in Idaho for people of every skill level. Through the power of exercise, we can all become our best selves as we are surrounded by the beautiful views that mother nature provides so freely for us here in this great state. So many good people are passionate about hiking because it’s such a great activity! But not everyone knows where to hike next. That’s where “Hike Idaho” comes in. By offering a nearly comprehensive list of the available hikes nearby we give you hikers a better way to be informed about your hiking decisions!

Hikes

Kelly Canyon Trail
“Explore this 3.7-mile loop trail near Ririe, Idaho. Generally considered a moderately challenging route, it takes an average of 1 h 42 min to complete. This is a popular trail for birding, hiking, and mountain biking, but you can still enjoy some solitude during quieter times of day. The best times to visit this trail are March through October. Dogs are welcome, but must be on a leash.”
-courtesy of alltrails.com
Cress Creek Trail
“Try this 1.3-mile loop trail near Ririe, Idaho. Generally considered an easy route, it takes an average of 41 min to complete. This is a popular trail for birding, hiking, and trail running, but you can still enjoy some solitude during quieter times of day. The best times to visit this trail are June through October. Dogs are welcome and may be off-leash in some areas.”
-courtesy of alltrails.com
Henrys Fork Greenway (North)
“Try this 2.0-mile out-and-back trail near St. Anthony, Idaho. Generally considered an easy route, it takes an average of 35 min to complete. This is a popular trail for birding, trail running, and walking, but you can still enjoy some solitude during quieter times of day. The best times to visit this trail are April through November. Dogs are welcome, but must be on a leash.”
-courtesy of alltrails.com

Reviews

There’s lots of hikers out there with lots of insight, here is the place to share! If you’re wanting to try a hike in the area, see what other people like you have liked from the experience! And once you’re done, make sure to leave your own review to add your thoughts on the hike! Kelly Canyon Trail
“Beautiful! Excellent views, strenuous exercise in parts, solitary (except for the cows!), and a great length for a casual hike.”
-Aeryle Kuehn(on alltrails.com)
Cress Creek Trail
“Beautiful view of the snake river, and doesn’t take too long to complete. There is a way to go which splits off from the main loop to go towards the top of the hill. The view from on top is gorgeous but going up and down is extremely steep, so come prepared to get a little dirty! Regardless, I would definitely go again!”
-Ben Davis
Henry’s Fork Greenway
“A really relaxing walk. Mostly paved at the beginning with some fun wooden bridges over parts of the river. Very relaxing once you get past the section by the freeway.”
-Ben Davis
Menan Butte
“It’s straight up for a while, but once you get to the top it is a great view to see the crater at the center. I would definitely say to take the trail which goes around the rim, it’s definitely worth it! Lots of good rocks to climb on and then you can see where the “r” actually is.”
-Ben Davis
